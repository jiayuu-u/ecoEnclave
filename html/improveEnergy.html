Improve Energy Efficiency

Find out some useful tips for improving your home’s energy efficiency

Building Material

Building material can have an impact on the housing energy efficiency. Although it depends on things like the size, location of house and the local climate, etc., choosing sustainable materials that are made from renewable materials use less energy in the manufacturing process. Sustainable building materials can also contribute to the home’s comfort and thermal efficiency, so that less energy will be needed on cooling and heating each year.

Household Insulation

Insulation plays a key role in house energy efficiency. According to Sustainability Victoria, insulation is considered as the most cost-effective way to improve home energy efficiency and make your home comfortable. Statistics show that a fully insulated home can reduce energy usage of heating and cooling by around 40 to 50 percent when compared to a non-insulated home.

Energy Efficient Appliances

Choosing the right appliances with better energy efficiency can also make your home greener. The majority of energy for a typical Australian home is on space conditioning. Apart from equipping homes with better insulation, using air conditioners with better energy ratings can also save a significant amount of energy.

Lighting

Lighting accounts for approximately 4 percent of Australian household energy consumption. Choosing the right light bulbs and using them correctly, such as turning the light off when you are away from home or using natural light whenever possible, will make your home more comfortable, energy efficient and significantly reduce energy usage.
