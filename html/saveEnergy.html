Energy Generation in Australia

An Unsustainable Path

Australia has one of the world’s most abundant coal reservations, both black and brown, which are mainly located in Queensland, New South Wales and Victoria. Coal contributes $40 billion annual export, the largest among other commodity exports. Australia’s energy generation also heavily relies on fossil fuels: 91 percent of energy is generated by fossil fuels with coal alone accounting for nearly 75 percent of Australia’s total energy generation.

Pollution

Burning fossil fuels can release sulfur dioxide, carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides and particulate matter into the atmosphere that causes air pollution. Air pollution has been linked to respiratory disease, cardiovascular disease and cancer. Children, pregnant women and elderly people are particularly vulnerable to air pollution.

Global Warming

The burning process also releases large amounts of greenhouse gases. Carbon dioxide accounts for more than 60 percent of all the greenhouse gases released. Carbon dioxide plays a huge role in climate change and global warming as it traps heat radiated from Earth back to space.
